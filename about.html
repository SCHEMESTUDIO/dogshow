About

What is The Dog Show?

The Dog Show is a live, shared dog-viewing experience. One dog appears at a time on a screen that everyone watches together. You can react in real-time chat, give bones to extend a dog's screen time, and if you're a premium member, upload your own dog to appear in the rotation.

That's it. That's the whole thing.

Why does this exist?

The internet has a lot of dogs on it. But it's mostly a solo experience — you scroll, you see a dog, you keep scrolling. The Dog Show is different because it's shared. Everyone sees the same dog at the same time. There's something weirdly special about watching a random poodle appear at 2am and seeing the chat go wild.

Who made this?

The Dog Show was built by Scheme Studio, a small creative studio that makes things on the internet. We like dogs and we like building things. This felt inevitable.

How does it work?

The show runs 24/7 on PartyKit, which handles real-time connections for chat, bone reactions, and the synchronized dog rotation. Community dog uploads are verified by AI to make sure they're actually dogs (you'd be surprised). Each dog gets a permanent certificate page with stats from their appearances.
